Educational Background
Earned a Bachelor of Science from the Wharton school and a Bachelor of Arts in philosophy from the School of Arts and Sciences at the University of Pennsylvania (Wikipedia)
Got his Juris Doctor degree from Harvard Law School (Wikipedia)

Career
Staff attorney in the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission as counsel to Senator Chuck Schumer on the U.S. Senate Banking COmmittee and as counsel and senior policy advisor for the Joint Economic Committee (Wikipedia)
Assistant law professor at the University of California, Irvine where he taught banking law, capital markets, and real estate finance (Wikipedia)
Joined the California State Senate in 2019, inspired to join politics after Trump’s “Muslim Ban” (Wikipedia)

Personal Information
Min is married to his wife Jan Stoever who is a professor of law at UC Irvine and they have three children together (Wikipedia)
He is a first-generation Korean American whose parents immigrated to California (Min Campaign Website)
