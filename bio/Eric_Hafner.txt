Career
Ran in the Republic primary for Hawaii’s Second Congressional District and lost; his issues include Hawaiian nationalism, medical marijuana advocacy, and criminal law reform despite being a New Jersey resident (Wikipedia)
In 2018 he ran in the Democratic party primary for U.S. House of Representatives elections in Oregon’s 3rd congressional district but he was a resident of New Jersey (Wikipedia)
He was imprisoned in 2019 for threatening to kill or injure elected officials, police officers, lawyers, judges, and their families connected to three court cases that went against him (Wikipedia)
