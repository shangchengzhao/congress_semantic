Juan Ciscomani

Educational Background
• Attended Pima Community College
• Bachelor’s of Art degree from the University of Arizona

Career
• Elected member of the U.S. House of Representatives from Arizona’s 6th District (2023); previously interned for a U.S. Representative and had a fellowship with a member of the U.S. House
• Elected member and Vice President of Outreach of the Tucson Hispanic Chamber of Commerce; served on the Arizona Civil Rights Advisory Board and Pima County Commission
• Supports securing the border and improving the quality of the immigration system, reducing inflation and cost of living, and protecting natural resources like water; does not support gun-control legislation

Personal Information
• Born in Hermosillo, Sonora, Mexico and first of his family to attend college
• Is a Protestant and has six children with his wife Laura
• Favorite activities include being a volunteer in his church and being a coach for his kids’ flag football teams
